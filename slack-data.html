How to Flag Tasks

If you encounter a task that seems broken (for example, the webpage doesn’t open, nothing loads, or there’s no code), follow these steps:

Step 1: Sometimes the issue is local. First, go through the "How to Handle Blockers" section and see if anything there resolves your issue.

Step 2: If the problem continues, drop the task link in the appropriate Slack channel. Ask if others are seeing the same thing.

Step 3: If others confirm it’s broken, wait for a lead or QA to advise. They’ll let you know what to do next.

How to Ask Questions in Slack

Please follow this process so that we can minimize the number of Slack messages for everyone:

1. Before asking questions, check the instructions or feedback notes. A good rule of thumb:

  • Look through the instructions
  • Review the canvas (:canvas: icon at the top of Slack)
  • Search in Slack (Ctrl + F)

2. Ask your questions in our internal channel.
The external channel is only for leads and QAs.

3. Slack question format:

  • Summary of your question/concern: Be specific and concise so it's easy to scan. Avoid walls of text.
    Once resolved, edit your post to include RESOLVED at the end.
  • In the thread:
    • Expand on your summary with more detail and context.
    • Include task links and screenshots to help others assist you faster.
  • Who to tag:
    • @iwqas – for general QA questions
    • Quality lead – for escalations
    • Campaign lead – for admin or broader escalations

How to Handle Blockers

If you hit a complete blocker: Let us know in the Slack channel immediately. While you wait for resolution, use the time wisely to deepen your understanding of the campaign.

Here’s how to stay productive:

  • Double-check all of your assigned tasks
  • Review campaign instructions and reference materials thoroughly
  • Help answer any unanswered questions in the Slack channel
  • If everything above is complete, follow the Downtime Policy

Initial Prompt Guide

Your initial prompt should be a concise overview of the target website. It should not go into detail of what the site should look like—this should be left for follow-up turns. We are not aiming for a perfect implementation in the first turn.

  • Question 3 from the FAQ covers this topic as well.
  • This thread from Rana has some more dos and don'ts
  • Joanne provided a workshop that goes into more detail about how we should structure our first prompt.
  • 🚫 DO NOT end the conversation explicitly in MTIW. e.g., “thanks, that is all”, “bye”, “this convo is over”
  • 🚫 DO NOT use a prompt for greetings! Especially that first one!
  • 🚫 DO NOT waste any message or turn on prompts that do not tackle the job at hand.
  • 🚫 DO NOT submit empty prompts.
  • 🚫 DO NOT pack the first prompt with all the aspects needed to achieve the goal—build it up over turns.
  • ✅ Prompt should be intentional
  • ✅ Actively aiming to achieve the webpage
  • ✅ Naturally written with no coding verbiage

Please react with :purple-check: if you read this post, and send us a prompt you are proud of here :arrow_down_small:

The instructions call for a “concise overview” of the target webpage. While the exact length of the initial prompt will vary with the complexity of the webpage, we can use the average length of a classic IW prompt to consider 1–2 paragraphs as ideal to capture the core functionality of most pages.

Flagging Tasks

If you encounter a task that is messed up in some way (e.g. if a webpage doesn't open, no code at all, loading issues), here are the steps:

  1. Sometimes, the issue may only be on your end. Go through the steps in the “blockers” section above and see if that solves your issue.
  2. If that doesn't work, post the link in the channel and someone else can verify if they are facing the same issue as you.
  3. If it is confirmed to be an error, the leads/QAs will inform you on the next steps.

Downtime Policy

There have been many questions lately as certain projects see intermittent pauses or gaps in task availability, and I wanted to confirm with you all that we currently do not have a downtime policy in effect with OpenAI.

This means that if you are assigned to a given project/campaign and there are no tasks to do, please escalate to your lead and OM and wait until there are available tasks again to resume work (and time tracking). Idle time is not paid time.

I know this is a change from how we previously operated, but due to:

  • Increased platform stability on Feather
  • The client's desire to measure ROI more closely

We can no longer cover paid downtime or guarantee a specific volume of work.

*Exceptions may apply for things like platform-wide outages lasting over 24 hours.

Rest assured: If the client does have alternative work for you while awaiting a new batch or campaign, we’ll find that opportunity and relay it to you!

If you encounter a complete blocker and cannot operate: Let us know in the channel. Meanwhile, focus on learning the campaign inside and out:

  • Ensure you reviewed all your tasks
  • Thoroughly review instructions and other resources
  • Help answer unanswered Slack questions
  • If all above is complete, follow the Downtime Policy
